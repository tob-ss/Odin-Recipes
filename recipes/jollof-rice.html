Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof rice.

A classic Nigerian dish traditionally served at parties and outgoings. Easy to make and too tasty. You can't go wrong.

Ingredients

  • 750g Long Grain Parboiled Rice
  • 500g Plum Tomatoes
  • 100g Tomato Paste
  • Vegetable Oil
  • 2 Large Onions
  • 3 Knorr Cubes
  • 2 Teaspoons Thyme
  • 2 Teaspoons Curry Powder
  • 2 Cups of Chicken Stock
  • Chicken (Whole Chicken, cut up into pieces, or drumsticks or chicken breast)
  • Salt and Pepper

Steps

  1. Wash and blend the fresh plum tomatoes. (Remove seeds to make mixture more smooth).
  2. Mix tomato paste with water to get a softer consistency and cut 1 onion into small pieces.
  3. Pour fresh tomato blend into a pot and cook at high heat until almost all the water has dried.
  4. Add vegetable oil, chopped onions and tomato paste to pot. Stir well.
  5. Fry at low heat and stir at short intervals until the oil has separated from the tomato puree.
  6. After all the water has dried, pour out the excess vegetable oil.
  7. Wash, cut the chicken and cook with thyme, knorr cubes and an onion (chopped).
  8. Parboil the rice, rinse parboiled rice and put in a sieve to drain.
  9. Pour the chicken stock and tomato stew into a pot and leave to boil.
  10. Add drained parboiled rice, curry powder, salt and pepper.
  11. Ensure that the water level is the same level as the rice.
  12. Cover the pot and leave to cook on low to medium heat.
  13. The rice is done when the water is dry, taste to confirm. If the rice needs more time to cook, add more water and reduce the heat to prevent burning
  14. Dig in and enjoy!
